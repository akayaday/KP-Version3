Bridge - Marriott Hotel

UX Research & Design

⇽ ⇾
At the MIT Mobile Experience Lab, the director, Federico Casalegno, held a class in partnership with Marriott Hotels, that was tasked with reconstructing the ideas of the lobby space. In accordance with the new demographic of tech savvy young professionals that the Marriott was reaching for, we sought to create a space in the lobby where guests could establish connections and meet other guests while earning loyalty points and brightening the lobby space in general. Hence, Bridge was Born. After playing with several different technologies, including Leap Motion and e-ink, we created an interactive table with NFC capabilities to load a site we developed onto your phone when placed on the table. We also added LED strips to illuminate as more people joined the table. For our detailed ethnographic research and documentation, check out our PDF document or Boston Globe article. Login is not required. Credit to Stephen Suen, Kaitlyn Greskoff, and Sandra Richter, my awesome teammates.
